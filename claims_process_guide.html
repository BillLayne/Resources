Immediate Steps After an Incident

First 30 minutes are critical

PRIORITY #1: SAFETY FIRST

Before anything else, ensure everyone is safe. Move to safety if possible, call 911 if there are injuries, and secure the scene. Claims can wait - lives cannot.

Step-by-Step Immediate Actions

Check for injuries, move to safety, call emergency services if required

Turn on hazard lights, set up flares if safe, avoid further damage

Required in NC if: Injuries, death, damage >$4,000, hit & run, impaired driver

Pro Photography Tips

Filing Your Claim

Time Limits Matter

File your claim as soon as possible. Most policies require "prompt notification."

  • • Immediately: Major accidents or significant damage
  • • Within 24 hours: Most incidents
  • • Check your policy: For specific requirements

Working with Adjusters

What is an Adjuster?

An adjuster is the insurance company's representative who investigates your claim, assesses damage, and determines settlement amounts. They work for the insurance company, not for you.

Do's and Don'ts

DO These Things

  • Be honest and factual

  • Keep detailed records

  • Provide documentation promptly

  • Ask questions if confused

  • Be present during inspections

DON'T Do These

  • Don't admit fault

  • Don't accept first offer

  • Don't sign without reviewing

  • Don't feel pressured

  • Don't dispose of evidence

Maximizing Your Settlement

Key Strategies

Documentation is Everything

  • Keep every receipt and document
  • Get multiple repair estimates
  • Document hidden damage
  • Track all expenses

Know Your Coverage

  • Read your policy thoroughly
  • Know your limits
  • Understand deductibles
  • Check for additional coverage

Negotiation Tips

  • • Don't accept the first offer: It's usually low
  • • Present evidence: Support with documentation
  • • Stay professional: Remain calm and factual
  • • Get it in writing: Verbal agreements aren't binding
  • • Consider all damages: Including diminished value

Timeline Expectations

Weeks 8-12: Negotiations

Factors Affecting Timeline

  • • Claim complexity and disputed fault
  • • Injury severity and ongoing treatment
  • • Documentation completeness
  • • Multiple parties involved
  • • Legal issues or attorney involvement

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Top 10 Costly Mistakes

1. Not Filing Promptly

Waiting too long can void your claim

2. Admitting Fault

Let investigators determine fault

3. Poor Documentation

Not taking enough photos or records

4. Accepting First Offer

Initial offers are often low

5. Not Reading Paperwork

Understand what you're signing

6. Disposing of Evidence

Keep damaged items as evidence

7. Skipping Medical Care

See a doctor even for minor injuries

8. Talking Too Much

Stick to facts, avoid speculation

9. Not Using Your Agent

Your agent is your advocate

10. Being Too Trusting

Verify everything, get it in writing

Different Types of Claims

Auto Insurance Claims

Collision Claims

  • • Police report usually required
  • • Multiple vehicle inspections
  • • Fault determination crucial
  • • Consider diminished value
  • • Rental car coverage important

Comprehensive Claims

  • • Weather reports for storm damage
  • • Police report for theft/vandalism
  • • Photos of all damage
  • • Inventory of stolen items
  • • Generally no fault issues

Homeowners Claims

Water Damage
  • • Stop water immediately
  • • Document before cleanup
  • • Prevent mold growth
  • • Keep mitigation receipts
Fire Damage
  • • Get fire department report
  • • Document smoke damage
  • • Consider living expenses
  • • Professional cleaning covered
Theft Claims
  • • File police report immediately
  • • Provide serial numbers
  • • Check for credit fraud
  • • Change locks and codes
